The Platypus

COMMON NAME

Platypus

SCIENTIFIC NAME

Ornithorhynchus anatinus

TYPE

Mammals

Diet

Carnivores

SIZE

Head and body, 15in; tail, 5in

WEIGHT

3 lbs

IUCN RED LIST STATUS

Nearly Threatened

CUTE

The platypus takes the appearance of a combination of three species - the duck (bill and webbed feet), beaver (tail), and otter (body and fur).

NOT CUTE

The platypus is nearly threatened to due human pollution, and other natural predators.

DANGEROUS

Males are the venomous ones. They have sharp stingers on the heels of their rear feet and can use them to defend themselves form predators!

TYPE

Mammals

Diet

Herbivores

SIZE

23.5 in - 33.5 in

WEIGHT

20 lbs

IUCN RED LIST STATUS

Vulnerable

CUTE

The koala bear, is actually not a bear at all; it is a marsupial, or pouched mammal. The female koala carries her baby in her pouch for about six months. When the baby is not in the pouch, it rides on its mother’s back or wraps it's arms around her belly, and will do this everyday until they are a year old!

NOT CUTE

The koalas are sadly vulnerable to human kind due to human civilization increase. Their homes are destroyed in order for people to build more buildings, cities, towns, etc.

DANGEROUS

The koala is a dangerous animal to humans as well. This is due to their claws are very posinous, and when they scratch humans it can be fatal.So.....

WARNING:

DO NOT MAKE THE KOALA UPSET.

The Slow Loris

COMMON NAME

Slow Loris

SCIENTIFIC NAME

Nycticebus

TYPE

Mammals

Diet

Omnivores

SIZE

Head and body, 15.0in

WEIGHT

9.3 oz

IUCN RED LIST STATUS

Threatened

CUTE

The adorable slow loris has huge eyes and a small body leaving many who come in contact with them to immediately fall inlove! The slow loris derrives from the south eastern parts of Asia, and they are found in forest habitats.

NOT CUTE

Sadly these cute creatures are poached heavily from their home territory of southeast Asia, leaving their species to be threatened. They are taken and sold all over the world as illegal pets.

DANGEROUS

They may be cute, but...they do have a very fatal poison that is from the skin of the bare part of their arms. The Loris' have a toxin that is obtained by licking a gland on their arm. This creates a toxic bite which is deadly to predators if bitten. The Loris toxin is also applied to the fur of their babies to help keep them safe from danger!

WARNING:

BEWARE THE BITE. DO NOT TOUCH WHEN ARMS ARE LIFTED.
